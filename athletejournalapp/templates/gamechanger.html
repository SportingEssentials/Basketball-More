Draymond Green: the piece that Completes the Warriors

The Trio

When discussing the impact of Draymond Green on the Golden State Warriors, it's impossible to ignore the dynamic trio formed by Green, Stephen Curry, and Klay Thompson. While Curry and Thompson are known for their scoring prowess, it is Draymond Green who creates opportunities for them to excel. Green's selfless play, exceptional screen-setting, and basketball IQ allow the Splash Brothers to shine and unleash their full offensive potential.

His unconventional screen-setting style has become a signature move, often catching defenders off guard and allowing his teammates to slip through for easy baskets. While these screens may raise eyebrows and draw the ire of opposing players and coaches, they undeniably contribute to the Warriors' offensive success. Green's ability to create space for his teammates showcases his understanding of the game and his commitment to making winning plays.

Intense Defense, Hunger to Win, and the Overlooked Art of Rebounding

When evaluating the impact of Draymond Green on the Golden State Warriors, it is essential to emphasize his contributions on the defensive end and his presence on the boards. While Green's offensive skills may not be as flashy as his teammates', his relentless defense and rebounding prowess are crucial to the team's success.

Draymond Green's defensive abilities often go unnoticed by casual fans who primarily focus on scoring highlights. However, Green recognizes the value of defense, as he once expressed in an interview, "Do you wish defense was seen in a sexier way? Being in good position or just staying vertical -- that's just as important potentially as hitting a splashy 3, but it's the 3 that ends up on a highlight reel."

In addition to his defensive contributions, Draymond Green's work on the boards should not be underestimated. Despite being undersized for his position, Green's tenacity and positioning allow him to secure rebounds against bigger opponents. In the 2022-23 season alone, Green accumulated an impressive 600 rebounds, showcasing his willingness to battle in the paint and secure possessions for his team.

Green's unheralded defensive and rebounding contributions exemplify his selfless approach to the game. While he may not be the leading scorer or the one featured in highlight reels, Green's ability to excel in the less glamorous aspects of basketball is what sets him apart. As he aptly put it, "Well, that would require people to know the game of basketball, and most people don't. Wishful thinking. Most people will never learn the game of basketball. They think they know, but yet don't have a clue. It is what it is. I enjoy being one of the not-so-many people that actually know the game - being in that rare category."

CJ McCollum, a talented shooting guard for the Portland Trail Blazers, recognizes Draymond Green's defensive prowess. In an interview, McCollum praised Green, stating, "He's an elite defender. He does so many things that don't show up on the stat sheet. He can guard multiple positions, disrupt passing lanes, and make timely rotations. He's one of those players who can change the game with his defense alone."

Draymond Green's impact on the defensive end is invaluable to the Warriors' success. His ability to contest shots, disrupt offensive plays, and secure crucial rebounds often goes unnoticed by casual observers. However, those who understand the game recognize his immense contributions and the significance of his defensive presence.

Dennis Schröder: A Game-Changing Presence for the Lakers

Redeeming Value and Making an Impact

When Dennis Schröder decided to return to the Los Angeles Lakers last summer, he had a clear mission in mind. Unlike his previous stint with the team in 2021, he wasn't focused on being the starting point guard or securing a lucrative contract extension. Schröder simply wanted a second chance with the purple and gold, and he proved his worth by delivering a stellar performance in the FIBA EuroBasket. This led to him signing a minimum contract with the Lakers and playing a significant role in their journey to the Western Conference finals. While the team fell short of their championship aspirations, Schröder's redemption on the court not only earned him recognition within the league but also solidified his place in the hearts of Lakers fans.

Impactful Energy and Stellar Defense

Throughout the season, Schröder's impact on both ends of the floor was undeniable. Whether or not he delivered in every game, his presence was felt by teammates, opponents, and fans alike. Known for his relentless energy, Schröder brought a level of intensity that elevated the team's performance. His commitment to lockdown defense and ability to disrupt opposing players made him a valuable asset in crucial moments. Moreover, his willingness to step up in clutch situations and deliver when it mattered most showcased his ability to be a game-changer for the Lakers.

Looking at the Stats

Dennis Schroder's performance in the 2022-23 season with the Los Angeles Lakers showcased his value as a skilled and impactful player. While the raw statistical numbers may not fully capture his impact, Schroder's contributions extended beyond the stat sheet, making him a game-changer for the team.

One of Schroder's key strengths lies in his ability to create scoring opportunities for himself and his teammates. His quickness and ball-handling skills allowed him to penetrate the defense, drawing attention and opening up opportunities for his teammates. Dennis Schroder's impact on the defensive end cannot be understated. Exceeding Expectations and Outplaying his Contract

For a player on a minimum contract deal, Schröder's production surpassed all expectations. He consistently contributed on both ends of the floor, coming up big when the team needed it most. His ability to close out games, create scoring opportunities, and provide tenacious defense made him a trusted player for Coach Darvin Ham. From the Play-In tournament to the playoffs, Schröder's performance played a significant role in the Lakers' success.

Moving on to the Toronto Raptors

While the future with the Lakers remains uncertain, Schröder recently agreed to a two-year, $26 million deal with the Toronto Raptors during the first day of free agency. This represents a solid payday for the talented guard who played on a veteran minimum deal last season. His time with the Lakers showcased his ability to be a major steal for any team, and the Raptors will now have the opportunity to benefit from his contributions. As Schröder embarks on this new chapter, he leaves the Lakers with appreciation and gratitude, expressing his sentiments on social media. Learning from These Players to Become a More Valuable Asset to your Team

Looking at the game-changers mentioned in this article, there are several key lessons that can be learned to improve your own game. Especially for smaller players, learning from Dray, Dennis, and Jose can significantly improve your chances of achieving your basketball dreams. Let's explore some of these lessons:

Embrace the Dirty Work:

Players who excel in rebounding, defense, and the relentless pursuit of victory demonstrate the importance of embracing the less glamorous aspects of the game. To improve your game, focus on developing skills in these areas and understanding their value to your team's success. Work on rebounding techniques, defensive positioning, and hustling for loose balls. By doing the dirty work, you can make a significant impact on your team's performance.

Be a Team Player:

Game-changers like Draymond Green, Jose Alvarado, and Dennis Schröder prioritize team success over individual accolades. They understand their roles within the team and contribute selflessly. To improve your game, embrace the concept of being a team player. Develop your understanding of the game, make smart decisions, and involve your teammates. Focus on setting screens, making good passes, and creating scoring opportunities for others. By putting the team first, you can enhance your own performance and contribute to a winning culture.

Embody Relentless Effort:

Players like Jose Alvarado and Dennis Schröder are known for their relentless effort and energy on the court. They hustle on every possession, dive for loose balls, and disrupt opponents with their intensity. To improve your game, adopt a similar mindset. Give your all on every play, both offensively and defensively. Develop a strong work ethic, maintain a high level of energy, and never give up on a play. Your relentless effort can inspire your teammates and create opportunities for success.

Develop Defensive Prowess:

Defense is a crucial aspect of the game, and players like Draymond Green excel in this area. They disrupt passing lanes, contest shots, and make it difficult for opponents to score. To improve your game, focus on enhancing your defensive skills. Work on your footwork, lateral quickness, and defensive positioning. Learn to anticipate opponents' moves, communicate effectively with teammates, and make timely rotations. By becoming a strong defender, you can make a significant impact on the game and earn the trust of your coaches and teammates.

Maintain a Hunger for Victory:

Game-changers like Draymond Green and Jose Alvarado possess an unwavering hunger for victory. They refuse to back down from challenges and inspire their teammates to strive for greatness. To improve your game, cultivate a strong desire to win. Develop a winning mentality, set challenging goals for yourself, and push yourself beyond your comfort zone. Embrace competition and use setbacks as fuel for improvement. Your hunger for victory can elevate your performance and motivate those around you.

Incorporating these lessons into your own game can help you become a more well-rounded and impactful player. Remember, it's not always about the spotlight or individual statistics. By embracing the dirty work, being a team player, embodying relentless effort, developing defensive prowess, and maintaining a hunger for victory, you can make a significant contribution to your team's success and elevate your own game.
